# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Men (and some women) aged 28–60 in South Florida researching hair restoration. They arrive skeptical: they've seen a dozen interchangeable clinic sites with teal gradients and stock smiles. Many browse at night on a phone. The job to be done: decide whether this clinic is credible and book a free consultation. Spanish-speaking market is large, but this build is English-only (v1).

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Miami Hair Transplant (miahairtransplant.com), a hair restoration clinic at 860 NW LeJeune Rd, Suite 402, Miami, FL 33126. Led by Dayami Venereo, APRN. Services: FUE hair transplant, PRP treatment, hairline restoration, eyebrow transplant, beard transplant, textured (afrocentric) hair transplant. Success = consultation bookings via phone (786) 952-6821 and the results gallery convincing skeptics.

## Brand Personality

Warm, precise, unhurried. Bronze Miami: the confidence of a master tailor's atelier crossed with a modern clinic. Not luxury-gold, not medical-teal. Evokes calm credibility and restored self-assurance.

## Anti-references

- Generic medical-teal/navy clinic templates with icon card grids.
- Dark "luxury" gold-serif hair clinic sites (the Turkey-clinic aesthetic).
- Cream/beige AI landing pages with eyebrow labels over every section.
- Stocky before/after collages with red arrows and starbursts.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over promises.** Results, credentials, and process specifics carry the argument; adjectives don't.
2. **Warmth is the brand color's job.** Copper/sienna carries Miami heat; backgrounds stay pure white or deep espresso.
3. **One idea per fold.** Long scroll, deliberate pacing, no competing CTAs.
4. **A person, not a procedure.** Photography and copy center the patient's face and confidence, not surgical hardware.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, full keyboard navigation, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation, semantic landmarks, alt text with editorial voice.
